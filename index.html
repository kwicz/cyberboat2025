What is an unconference?

Unconferences reduce the usual emphasis on formal workshops and talks of the traditional conference structure and instead focus on informal connections. What does this mean? We threw out the schedule! (Okay, not all of it — you do have to show up on time or the boat will leave without you.) But, there's no talk tracks. No CFP. Just people and fun stuff to do — this con is ALL HallwayCon.

root@hackboat:~# ./join_us --for=collaboration --and=hacking

Why an Unconference?

The best parts of any conference are the spontaneous hallway conversations and connections made between sessions. Hackboat is designed to maximize these meaningful interactions in a relaxed, pressure-free environment.

Why on a Boat?

The confined yet moving space of a boat creates a unique environment that encourages focused participation and networking. Plus, the changing backdrop of the Willamette River provides inspiration and a natural conversation starter.

Who Should Attend?

Security researchers, penetration testers, blue team defenders, security architects, privacy advocates, and anyone passionate about information security. From seasoned professionals to curious newcomers, all are welcome aboard.

What to Expect

An open format for organic discussions, impromptu demos, collaborative problem-solving, and networking with peers. Bring your laptop, ideas, and enthusiasm. Leave your slides and formal presentations at home.

Location

We'll meet at 110 SE Caruthers St, Portland, Oregon, 97214 (by OMSI) at 9:00 AM. The boat will depart promptly at 10:00 AM and return to the same location by 2:00 PM.

Boarding Information

Please arrive by 9:00 AM for check-in and networking. The boat will not wait for late arrivals. Bring photo ID matching your registration name, your laptop, and your curiosity. We'll provide food, drinks, and Wi-Fi.
